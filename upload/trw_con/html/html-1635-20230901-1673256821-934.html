The GIT Design Awards 2013 has been greatly and successfully organized with deserved dignity, under the title “Jewelry Design Inspired by Cubism”, and presided by the deputy Prime Minister.

The 7th GIT Design Awards in 2013 attracted a total of 438 designs from designers in Thailand and other Asian countries. Among them, there were 83 men jewelry designs and 355 female jewelry designs. A total of 103 designs from foreign designers include those from India, Malaysia, Singapore, Vietnam, the Philippines, Japan and Hong Kong. The design prequalification round was held on last Friday, 28th June, and the expert committee has selected a total of 30 designs from both categories. The qualified designs were exhibited alongside the works designed by students from the institute’s design course. In this regards, the committee finally selected 6 from 30 prequalified designs to be used in actual production of jewelries and brought into the final round of the Jewelry Design Awards. Besides, GIT has offered an extra award i.e. the Popular Design Award which is based on votes from interested persons among the 30 prequalified designs. On this occasion, there were a total of 438 votes from the participants.

The exhibition of jewelry designs was organized for 2 days. The final round of the competition was held on Friday 8th November 2013 at the Fashion Hall on 1st floor of the Siam Paragon department store. The event activities include an exhibition of designs prequalified for the final round competition and there are numbers of event participants including relevant persons from government sector, entrepreneurs, designers, and other interested persons.

The institute invited Mr. Niwatthamrong Boonsongpaisan, the deputy Prime Minister and the Minister of the Ministry of Commerce, to preside the opening ceremony and present awards to the winners. On Saturday 9th November 2013, there was a fashion show of jewelry designs by students of the institute’s handmade jewelry design course, performance shows, and also the pop quiz competition.

Furthermore, there is an extra award, which receives a cash award of 1,000 USD, called the Popular Design Award 2013. This award is based on number of votes from participants for their favorite design via www.git.or.th, with the total number of 438 votes. The winner who received the highest votes is Mr. Manop Boudang, with the design work named Astroid, which was also awarded the winner in the competition. One of the participants in the Popular Design Award voting, Ms. Pattaranit Premsanga, also received a special prize, which is a piece of diamond - embedded jewelry, from L.S. Jewelry Group. At the event, all 30 prequalified designers were awarded a certificate by the institute’s director. Also, a sapphire-embedded golden ring from P.K. Gems and Gold Co.,Ltd., was given via a lucky draw for the mass media journalists participating in the event. The lucky winner is Mr. Jatukupa Duangpimharn, a journalist from the 5 entertain - page 1 program of the Royal Thai Army Radio and Television Channel 5.
