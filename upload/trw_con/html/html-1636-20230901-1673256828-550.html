The 6th Gem and Jewelry Design Contest GIT Gem and Jewelry Design Award 2012 Under the Topic of "Jewelry design inspired by Thumbelina the Fairy tale"

GIT is the principal agency responsible for developing, promoting, and supporting the Thai gem and jewelry industry to be competitive in the global market. GIT plays an important role in analyzing, inspecting, and assuring the quality of gem, jewelry, and precious metal products. The institution also conducts research and development comprehensively, establishes the in-depth database on gem and jewelry marketing for disseminating data to the industrial sector as well as develops the quality of personnel in the Thai gem and jewelry industry so that they can be a cogwheel that drives the country’s gem and jewelry industry to be competitive in the global trading stage sustainably.

Creating added value for products through design is considered as a key to success in the gem and jewelry business which has become increasingly important. Therefore, GIT has provided opportunities for new-generation designers to show their power of creativity by organizing the exhibition to display jewelry design students’ work for 8 years consecutively, together with holding the gem and jewelry design contest for 6 consecutive years. In this year, the contest is under the topic of “Jewelry design inspired by Thumbelina the Fairy tale.” Thumbelina is a tiny girl who was born from a tulip and was kidnapped from her home. She took exciting adventure which made this story colorful. This story is, therefore, used as the theme for jewelry design. More importantly, it is congruent with the trend of this year and next year. GIT aims to support youths and new-generation designers to have a stage where they can step from their frameworks to new ideas. Moreover, this contest enables Thai and foreign designers to exchange their attitudes and viewpoints. This encourages Thai designers to be confident in showing their work more challengingly.

In the 6th Gem and Jewelry Design Contest, there were 540 drawing designs submitted by designers from Thailand and neighbor countries such as Malaysia, Singapore, and Hong Kong. These designs include 387 designs from university students and 153 designs from general people. The first round in which the drawing designs were judged by the qualified committee was held on August 31st, 2012. Thirty drawing designs submitted by contestants in both categories were selected to be displayed with design works of the institution’s jewelry design students. Then the committee selected 6 distinctive designs from 30 designs passing the first round to be produced as real jewelry. One of those designs is the design of a Malaysian contestant. In addition, GIT offered another special prize which is Popular Design Award by allowing people who are interested in the contest to cast their votes for their favorite designs among 30 designs passing the first round through the GIT website. There were 1,600 voters.

In the final round held on Saturday 24 November 2012, there were a large numbger of participants including entrepreneurs, designers, and general people. The institute invited Mr. Siripol Yodmuangcharoen, Chairman of the Board of Executive Directors of the Gem and Jewelry Institute of Thailand (Public Organization), to preside over this final round.

In addition, there is another special prize of 1,000 USD in cash. It is called Popular Design Award 2012 which provides a chance for Thais and foreigners to cast their votes for their favorite designs through www.git.or.th. There were 1,600 voters. The contestant who gained the highest votes is Mr, Sirisak Dontaolek from Suan Sunandha Rajabhat University with the design name of “The Thumbelina.” The voter for Popular Design who received a gold pendant from Li Seng Jewelry is Ms. Sidalai Kanotai.

   

 

Apart from the atmosphere of the final round, there was also the exhibition zone which displayed drawing designs including 30 design works of the institute’s jewelry design students and 30 design works of the contestants. Moreover, there were Harp’s Music, Statue Busker and quiz games to win prizes throughout the event.
